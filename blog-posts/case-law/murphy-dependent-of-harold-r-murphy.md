# West Virginia Supreme Court Affirms Denial of Workers' Compensation
Dependent Benefits in Asbestos-Related Death Claim, Finding Board of
Review's Weighing of Medical Evidence Not Clearly Wrong

**Court:** Supreme Court of Appeals of West Virginia **[Editorial Note: Case No. 23-ICA-447 suggests this case may have originated in the Intermediate Court of Appeals of West Virginia rather than the Supreme Court of Appeals. The deciding court has not been verified against the official reporter.]**
**Date:** March 19, 2025 --- 2025 WL 855516

---

## Introduction

Harold R. Murphy (deceased) allegedly worked in conditions exposing him
to asbestos. He developed severe asbestosis with right heart failure.
Linda Murphy, as his dependent, sought workers' compensation death
benefits from his employer, St. Gobain Ceramics & Plastics, Inc. The central question was whether the Board of Review properly weighed conflicting medical evidence about whether asbestos exposure was a material contributing cause of Harold Murphy's death.

## The Procedural Background

The claim administrator denied dependent benefits. The Board of Review
affirmed the denial. The Intermediate Court of Appeals affirmed the
Board of Review. Linda Murphy petitioned the Supreme Court of Appeals.

## The Legal Issue at Stake

Whether the Occupational Pneumoconiosis Board properly weighed the
medical evidence regarding whether asbestos was a material contributing
cause of Harold Murphy's death.

## Arguments Presented to the Court

Murphy (Petitioner): Argued the Board improperly weighed expert
opinion showing asbestos fibers in lung tissue caused asbestosis and
right heart failure.

St. Gobain/Employer (Respondent): Argued overwhelming evidence
supported the denial of benefits.

## The Court's Holding

Affirmed. The Board of Review's factual findings were not clearly wrong.

## Detailed Analysis and Reasoning

The court applied the deferential standard of review, under which the
Board of Review's factual findings are not disturbed unless clearly
wrong. The court found no reversible error in the Board's weighing of
the medical evidence.

## Practical Significance for Legal Practitioners

This decision illustrates the high bar dependents face when challenging Board of Review factual findings in workers' compensation asbestos death claims. The "clearly wrong" standard of review means that even when medical evidence appears to support the claimant---such as asbestos fibers found in lung tissue---the Board's decision to credit contrary evidence or find insufficient causal connection will be upheld unless plainly erroneous.

Key practice points include:
- In West Virginia workers' compensation occupational disease cases, the Board of Review's factual findings are reviewed under the deferential "clearly wrong" standard, making appellate reversal difficult
- Dependents seeking death benefits in asbestos cases must present medical evidence that conclusively establishes asbestos exposure as a material contributing cause of death, not merely the presence of asbestos-related disease
- Building the strongest possible medical record at the administrative level is critical, because appellate courts will not reweigh evidence or substitute their judgment for the Board's on questions of medical causation
