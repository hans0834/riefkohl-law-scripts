Puerto Rico courts hold trustees to strict fiduciary standards under the Trust Law (Ley de Fideicomisos, Law 219-2012). Understanding how courts have ruled on breach of fiduciary duty claims is essential for trustees, beneficiaries, and their attorneys. # Puerto Rico Trust Law Cases: Breach of Fiduciary Duty Summaries

## Case 1: Alfredo Ramirez de Arellano v. Banco Popular de Puerto Rico

### Headline
Court of Appeals Decision on Breach of Contract and Fiduciary Duties in Trust Fund Investment and Management by Commercial Bank

### Court
Puerto Rico Court of Appeals

### Date
2025 (Westlaw Citation: 2437534)

### Summary of Relevant Facts
Alfredo Ramirez de Arellano, as trustee and beneficiary of a trust, engaged Banco Popular de Puerto Rico to manage and invest trust assets. The trust held funds intended for investment purposes. The bank was responsible for exercising prudent investment practices and maintaining fiduciary standards in managing the trust portfolio. Over the course of the trust's administration, the bank allegedly engaged in imprudent investment practices and failed to properly diversify the trust's assets.

### Procedural Background
The case was brought before the Puerto Rico Court of Appeals as an appellate matter. The plaintiff-appellant challenged a lower court determination regarding the bank's liability for breach of contract and breach of fiduciary duty in its management of trust assets.

### Main Controversies
The primary issues in dispute were: (1) whether the bank breached its contractual obligations to the trust in its investment management practices; (2) whether the bank breached its fiduciary duties owed to the trust and its beneficiaries; (3) the proper measure of damages for imprudent investments; and (4) the standards applicable to commercial banks acting as trustees under Puerto Rico law.

### Position of the Parties
The plaintiff/appellant Alfredo Ramirez de Arellano contended that Banco Popular breached its fiduciary duties and contractual obligations by making imprudent investments and failing to maintain adequate diversification of trust assets. The bank maintained that its investment decisions were reasonable under the circumstances and that it complied with applicable standards for trust management. The bank also argued that any losses resulted from market conditions rather than imprudent management.

### Holding/Decision
The Court of Appeals found in favor of the plaintiff, determining that Banco Popular de Puerto Rico breached both its contractual obligations and fiduciary duties to the trust in its management and investment practices.

### Reasons for the Decision
The court held that commercial banks serving as trustees are held to a high fiduciary standard consistent with Puerto Rico's Trust Law (Ley de Fideicomisos). The bank's investment practices failed to meet the prudent investor standard required by law. The court found that the bank did not adequately diversify the trust's portfolio and made investment decisions that exposed the trust to undue risk. The court also determined that the bank's conduct constituted a material breach of the contractual terms governing trust management. Damages were awarded to compensate the trust for losses resulting from the imprudent investments.

## Case 2: Fideicomiso Para Ciencia, Tecnologia E Investigacion Para Puerto Rico v. Thomas Forest Farb

### Headline
Appeal of Breach of Contract and Fiduciary Duty Claims Against Trustee of Public Trust for Scientific and Technological Research

### Court
Puerto Rico Court of Appeals

### Date
June 30, 2014

### Summary of Relevant Facts
The Fideicomiso Para Ciencia, Tecnologia E Investigacion Para Puerto Rico (Trust for Science, Technology and Research in Puerto Rico) is a public trust established with charitable purposes to advance scientific and technological research in Puerto Rico. Thomas Forest Farb served as a trustee of this public trust. A dispute arose regarding the management of trust assets and the trustee's performance of his fiduciary duties. The case involved allegations that the trustee breached his fiduciary obligations in administering the trust and its resources for the stated charitable purposes.

### Procedural Background
The case was appealed to the Puerto Rico Court of Appeals under case number KAC2012-0126. The appellate court reviewed the lower court's determinations regarding whether the trustee had breached his fiduciary duties and contractual obligations to the trust.

### Main Controversies
The central issues were: (1) whether Thomas Forest Farb breached his fiduciary duties as trustee of the public trust; (2) whether the trustee breached the express terms of the trust agreement in administering trust assets; (3) allegations of political discrimination in the trustee's administration of the trust; (4) the proper standard of review for trustee conduct in public charitable trusts; and (5) the appropriate remedies for breach of fiduciary duty in the context of a public trust.

### Position of the Parties
The Fideicomiso contended that Farb breached his fiduciary duties in managing the trust, failed to deploy resources in accordance with the trust's charitable purposes, and allowed political considerations to influence trust administration decisions. Farb argued that his actions as trustee were within the scope of his discretionary authority, that he complied with the trust terms, and that his decisions regarding resource allocation reflected legitimate judgment calls regarding trust management. Farb also disputed the political discrimination allegations.

### Holding/Decision
The Court of Appeals determined that the trustee had breached his fiduciary duties to the public trust in certain respects, particularly regarding the allocation and use of trust resources for the stated charitable and research purposes.

### Reasons for the Decision
The court held that trustees of public charitable trusts are subject to strict fiduciary standards under Puerto Rico law. The court found that the trustee failed to adequately deploy trust resources toward the trust's stated purposes of advancing scientific and technological research. The court also found that the trustee's decision-making process was influenced by considerations outside the scope of proper trust administration. The court held that the trustee had a clear obligation to use trust assets in furtherance of the charitable mission and that deviations from this obligation constitute breaches of fiduciary duty. The court determined that remedies were warranted to ensure proper future administration of the trust.

## Case 7: Narvaez Maldonado v. Banco Popular de Puerto Rico

Appeal Concerning Trust Fund Administration and Fiduciary Responsibilities of Commercial Bank Trustee

### Court
Puerto Rico Court of Appeals

### Date
June 22, 2015

### Summary of Relevant Facts
Narvaez Maldonado was a beneficiary of a trust administered by Banco Popular de Puerto Rico. The trust held assets that were subject to the bank's management and investment responsibilities. The beneficiary brought claims alleging that the bank failed to properly administer the trust funds, mismanaged investments, and breached its fiduciary duties. The dispute centered on the bank's investment decisions, asset allocation, account management, and overall stewardship of trust assets.

### Procedural Background
The case was appealed to the Puerto Rico Court of Appeals under case numbers KPE2012-1448 and KLCE20150641. The appellate court reviewed the lower court's findings regarding whether Banco Popular breached its fiduciary duties as trustee and what remedies were appropriate.

### Main Controversies
The primary issues included: (1) whether the bank met the applicable standard of care in managing and investing trust assets; (2) whether investment decisions were prudent and appropriate; (3) whether the bank breached its fiduciary duties to the beneficiary; (4) whether the bank's asset allocation was reasonable and diversified; (5) the proper measure of damages for any fiduciary breach; and (6) the standards applicable to commercial banks serving as trustees under Puerto Rico law.

### Position of the Parties
Narvaez Maldonado argued that Banco Popular breached its fiduciary duties through imprudent investments, inadequate diversification, and poor management of trust assets, resulting in diminished trust value and lost returns. Banco Popular contended that it exercised reasonable care in managing the trust, that investment decisions were prudent under the circumstances, and that any investment losses resulted from market conditions rather than imprudent management.

### Holding/Decision
The Court of Appeals found that Banco Popular de Puerto Rico breached its fiduciary responsibilities to the trust beneficiary in certain respects regarding the administration and investment of trust funds.

### Reasons for the Decision
The court held that commercial banks serving as trustees are subject to the prudent investor standard under Puerto Rico's Trust Law. The court found that the bank failed to meet this standard in specific investment decisions or in its overall investment strategy. The court determined that the bank's conduct constituted material breaches of fiduciary duty, warranting damages in favor of the beneficiary. The decision reflects rigorous application of fiduciary standards to commercial trustees and recognition that banks are held to high standards of care in managing trust assets.

## Summary Notes

These cases demonstrate Puerto Rico courts' rigorous application of fiduciary duty standards to trustees, including commercial banks, in the administration of both family trusts and public charitable trusts. Key recurring themes include:

1. **Prudent Investor Standard**: Courts consistently apply a high prudent investor standard to trustees making investment decisions.

2. **Accounting Requirements**: Trustees have mandatory obligations to maintain accurate accounts and render accounting to beneficiaries.

3. **Commercial Bank Trustees**: Banks are held to the same high fiduciary standards as individual trustees, with particular scrutiny of investment decisions and diversification practices.

4. **Trust Termination and Distribution**: Courts carefully scrutinize trustee conduct regarding trust termination decisions and the proper allocation and distribution of trust assets to beneficiaries.

5. **Public Trusts**: Trustees of public charitable trusts face heightened fiduciary standards, with courts ensuring that trust assets are deployed consistent with the trust's charitable purposes.

6. **Inter-Vivos Trusts**: Trusts created during the settlor's lifetime receive protection through fiduciary standards, particularly regarding asset management and co-trustee relationships.

7. **Complex Assets**: When trusts hold corporate interests or other complex assets, courts carefully review trustee decisions regarding valuation, allocation, and distribution of these assets to beneficiaries.
