### Long Bio (300 words)

Hans E. Riefkohl is the founder and managing member of Riefkohl Law (Riefkohl LLC), a virtual law firm based in San Juan, Puerto Rico. The firm provides legal counsel in trusts and estate planning, Act 60 tax incentives, business formation, government contracts, and creditor-side bankruptcy representation.

Hans brings experience across law and accounting to his practice. Before founding the firm, he served as an associate at DLA Piper, one of the world's largest law firms, where he worked on complex corporate transactions and regulatory matters. He later served as corporate counsel at LUMA Energy, Puerto Rico's power transmission and distribution operator, where he managed government contracts, procurement, and regulatory compliance.

His experience at both BigLaw and in-house positions gives him a practical, business-oriented approach to legal problem-solving.

Hans graduated summa cum laude from the University of Puerto Rico School of Law and holds a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration from Villanova University. He is admitted to the Puerto Rico Bar, the District of Columbia Bar, the U.S. District Court for the District of Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Court of Appeals for the First Circuit. He clerked for Chief Justice Maite D. Oronoz Rodriguez at the Supreme Court of Puerto Rico and for then-Judge Gustavo A. Gelpi (now of the First Circuit) at the U.S. District Court for the District of Puerto Rico.

Riefkohl Law operates on a flat-fee, productized pricing model, providing clients with cost certainty from the start. The firm serves three primary audiences: Act 60 decree holders and high-net-worth individuals relocating to Puerto Rico, local businesses and entrepreneurs, and families needing estate planning services. All services are available in both English and Spanish.
