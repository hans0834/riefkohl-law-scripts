## FLAGGED STATEMENTS — Potential Issues

### FLAG 1: Surviving Spouse as Forced Heir — Inconsistency Within Files
**Statements:** RES-001, RES-015, RES-051 (resources-fix.html, Forced Heirship page)
**Issue:** The forced heirship page at `/resources-forced-heirship` states the legítima is "Reserved for forced heirs (descendants)" and lists forced heirs as "Descendants: Children are the primary forced heirs." The surviving spouse is NOT listed as a forced heir on this page (RES-015, RES-016). However, other pages within the same file (RES-029, RES-030, RES-037, RES-042) and legal-content-fixes.html (LEGAL-013, LEGAL-014, LEGAL-015) correctly state the surviving spouse IS a forced heir under Ley 55-2020.
**Risk:** CRITICAL. The /resources-forced-heirship page omits the surviving spouse from the forced heir list, which is a material omission that could mislead clients into estate plans that fail to account for the spouse's legítima share.
**Recommendation:** Update the forced heirship page (RES-015) to explicitly include the surviving spouse as a forced heir.

### FLAG 2: Pricing Manipulation in Services Page Transform
**Statement:** HOME-002
**Issue:** The homepage-services-redesign.html JavaScript code takes the original trust tier prices (e.g., "$7,500 - $12,000"), extracts the low end, multiplies by 0.25, and displays "From $1,875." This algorithmically reduces displayed prices by 75% from the original content. If the original Squarespace page content reflects actual pricing, the displayed price is misleadingly low. If the 0.25 multiplier was intentional to reflect a different pricing model, this needs verification.
**Risk:** CRITICAL. Displaying manipulated prices that are 75% lower than the actual fee structure could constitute deceptive advertising and violate PR Bar ethics rules on fee advertising.
**Recommendation:** Verify whether the "From $X" prices match actual minimum fees. If not, correct the multiplier or display actual starting prices.

### FLAG 3: OBBBA Signing Date
**Statement:** LEGAL-021
**Issue:** The code states "One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed into law July 2025." This needs verification — if the signing date is inaccurate, the estate tax exemption amount ($15M) and permanency claim may also be wrong.
**Risk:** CRITICAL. If the OBBBA was not signed or has different provisions than stated, the $15M exemption figure and "permanent" characterization are wrong, affecting NRNC estate planning guidance.
**Recommendation:** Verify the exact signing date and provisions of the OBBBA.

### FLAG 4: HB 505 Provisions — Multiple Claims Need Verification
**Statements:** LEGAL-002, LEGAL-003, LEGAL-004, LEGAL-005, LEGAL-024, LEGAL-029
**Issue:** Multiple critical claims about HB 505: (1) approved February 2026, (2) 0% rate ends for applications after Dec 31, 2026, (3) 4% rate replaces it, (4) existing holders grandfathered, (5) program extended through 2055, (6) 6-year prior residency restriction added. These are all recent legislative changes that need verification against the actual enacted text.
**Risk:** CRITICAL. If any of these provisions are inaccurate, the firm is providing materially wrong tax advice on its website.
**Recommendation:** Verify all HB 505 provisions against the enacted statute.

### FLAG 5: Municipal and Property Tax Exemption Percentages
**Statements:** LEGAL-025, LEGAL-026

**Recommendation:** Verify against current Act 60 statutory text.

### FLAG 6: Bid Protest Deadlines

**Risk:** CRITICAL. A client relying on an incorrect deadline could lose their right to protest.

### FLAG 7: Trust Duration — 70 Years
**Statements:** RES-028, RES-032

### FLAG 8: Asset Protection Comparative Claim

### FLAG 9: NRNC Unified Credit Amount

### FLAG 10: "Over a Decade of Experience" Claim
