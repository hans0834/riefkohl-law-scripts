# Riefkohl Law — SEO & Digital Marketing Audit
## Date: March 13, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Riefkohl Law (riefkohllaw.com) has a technically solid Squarespace-hosted website with strong foundational elements: comprehensive JSON-LD schema markup (LegalService, LocalBusiness, Article), proper hreflang implementation for bilingual content, clean URL structure, correct HTTPS redirect chains, and a substantial blog with 300+ posts. The site's technical bones are above average for a solo law firm.

**However, the site has several critical issues that are almost certainly preventing it from converting organic visibility into rankings and leads.** The most damaging finding is that the site has **zero presence on Google Business Profile and zero listings on any major legal directory** (Avvo, Justia, FindLaw, Martindale-Hubbell, Super Lawyers). In 8 competitive keyword searches, riefkohllaw.com did not appear in the top 10 results for any target query. For a law firm dependent on local search, this is an emergency-level gap.

The second critical issue is a **site-wide duplicate H1 tag problem**: every single page on the site — homepage, practice areas, blog posts, contact — has the identical H1: "Explore Our Legal Analysis." This means Google has no unique H1 signal for any page's primary keyword. Similarly, most pages share an identical generic meta description, eliminating any keyword-specific SERP differentiation.

The third strategic concern is **content misallocation**: the most recent blog content is overwhelmingly focused on government contracts case summaries, which is the firm's lowest-revenue practice area. Meanwhile, trusts & estate planning ($7,500–$50,000+ per engagement) and Act 60 advisory ($5,000+) — the firm's highest-value services — have relatively less recent content investment.

**What's working well:** The site has excellent schema markup, a clean bilingual architecture with hreflang, strong E-E-A-T signals (DLA Piper, LUMA Energy, judicial clerkships, 15+ years experience, $100M+ in transactions), proper CTAs on every page, and a large content library that demonstrates topical authority in government contracts. The flat-fee pricing model is a competitive differentiator.

**Strategic priorities for the next 90 days:** (1) Claim and optimize Google Business Profile immediately, (2) fix duplicate H1 and meta description issues across all pages, (3) fix the confirmed staging URL leak, (4) create directory listings on Avvo/Justia/FindLaw/Martindale, (5) shift blog content strategy toward trusts, estate planning, and Act 60 topics, (6) populate the empty /resources page with lead magnets.

Personalized legal counsel in English and Spanish." appears on the homepage, services, about, estate planning, act 60, business formation, government contracts, and multiple blog posts. Google may rewrite these, but having unique, keyword-targeted meta descriptions with CTAs significantly improves click-through rates. **Fix: Write unique meta descriptions for every page and blog post**, each under 160 characters, including the primary keyword and a call to action. Priority: CRITICAL. Effort: Moderate.

Effort: Quick Win (5-minute fix).

## High Priority Improvements

6. **Blog content is heavily skewed toward government contracts — the lowest-revenue practice area.** The 12 most recent blog posts are ALL government contracts case summaries (both English and Spanish). While the broader blog library (300+ posts) covers trusts, bankruptcy, and corporate topics, the recent publishing cadence is sending topicality signals to Google that this is primarily a government contracts firm. The firm's highest-revenue services (trusts at $7,500–$50,000+, Act 60 at $5,000+) need proportionally more content. **Fix: Shift publishing ratio to at least 40% trusts/estate planning, 30% Act 60/corporate, 20% government contracts, 10% bankruptcy.** Priority: HIGH. Effort: Major Project (ongoing).

7. **Blog content loads entirely via JavaScript/API.** The blog uses a custom `rl-blog-dashboard` JavaScript application that fetches posts from `/blog?format=json`. Blog post content does not appear in the initial HTML — it loads asynchronously after JavaScript execution. While Googlebot can render JavaScript, this creates crawlability risk: if rendering fails or is delayed, Google sees empty pages. The WebFetch tool consistently returned only the dashboard skeleton, not article content. **Fix: Investigate whether Squarespace serves the actual blog post content in the initial HTML (pre-render). If the blog dashboard overlay is hiding server-rendered content, this is a major SEO risk. Consider whether the custom dashboard is worth the trade-off, or if standard Squarespace blog templates would be more crawlable.** Priority: HIGH. Effort: Major Project.

8. **No reviews anywhere.** Zero Google reviews, zero Avvo reviews, zero testimonials visible on the website. Social proof is a critical trust signal for legal services. Competitors have dozens to hundreds of reviews. **Fix: (1) After claiming Google Business Profile, implement a review request workflow for completed engagements. (2) Add a testimonials section to the homepage and practice area pages. (3) Request reviews on Avvo and Google from past clients.** Priority: HIGH. Effort: Moderate.

9. **No H2/H3 heading hierarchy on any page.** Every page shows only the duplicate H1 plus generic H2s ("Browse by Practice Area," "Need Legal Guidance?"). There are no content-specific H2 or H3 subheadings within practice area pages or blog posts (in the server-rendered HTML). This eliminates heading-based keyword signals and featured snippet opportunities. **Fix: Add keyword-rich H2/H3 subheadings to all practice area pages and blog posts.** For example, the /puerto-rico-trusts page should have H2s like "Types of Puerto Rico Trusts," "Irrevocable Trusts Under Law 219-2012," "Trust vs. Will in Puerto Rico," "Asset Protection Through Puerto Rico Trusts." Priority: HIGH. Effort: Moderate.

10. **The /resources page is empty/near-empty.** The page title is just "Resources — Riefkohl LLC" and the content is the same blog dashboard skeleton with no dedicated resource content. This is a wasted opportunity for lead magnets, guides, FAQs, and pillar content. **Fix: Populate with downloadable resources:**
    - "Guide to Puerto Rico Trusts Under Law 219-2012" (PDF lead magnet)
    - "Act 60 Tax Incentives Checklist for Relocators" (PDF lead magnet)
    - "Puerto Rico Estate Planning FAQ"
    - "Business Formation in Puerto Rico: Step-by-Step Guide"
    - Links to relevant statutes (Law 219-2012, Act 60, PR Civil Code)
    Priority: HIGH. Effort: Major Project.

11. **Image alt text appears missing or empty across the site.** No meaningful alt text was detected on any page. Images (logo, attorney photos, icons) all appear to lack descriptive alt attributes. **Fix: Add keyword-relevant alt text to all images, especially the attorney photo ("Hans Riefkohl Puerto Rico trust attorney"), practice area images, and the firm logo.** Priority: HIGH. Effort: Quick Win.

### 7. Risk Assessment & Emerging Issues

1. **Google Algorithm Vulnerability — JavaScript Rendering Dependency:** The site's custom blog dashboard architecture means ALL content (practice areas AND blog posts) is loaded via JavaScript. If Google's rendering budget is exhausted or delayed, pages may be indexed with only the skeleton HTML. This is the site's biggest technical risk. Google has improved JS rendering, but for a site with 619 URLs, rendering budget is a real concern.

2. **AI Content Detection Risk — LOW to MODERATE.** The government contracts blog posts appear to be case summaries following a consistent formula (case name + holding + analysis). The repetitive structure (all 12 recent posts follow the same template) could theoretically flag AI detection, but case summaries are inherently formulaic in legal writing. The bigger risk is that the content lacks unique practical insights — the posts read as academic summaries rather than practitioner advice.

3. **Squarespace Platform Limitations:**
   - Cannot add custom server-side rendering
   - Limited control over HTML output (the duplicate H1 issue likely stems from the blog dashboard template)
   - No access to server logs for crawl analysis
   - Limited custom schema implementation (though current schema is good)
   - No .htaccess or edge-level redirects
   - No server-side caching controls
   - **Recommendation: Do NOT migrate away from Squarespace at this time.** The platform limitations are real but manageable. Focus on fixing content and marketing gaps first, which are far more impactful than platform changes.

4. **Link Profile Concerns — Likely Very Thin.** The firm has zero directory listings, which means virtually zero authoritative backlinks. The only external link found was Hans Riefkohl's profile on mpmlawpr.com (former employer). Without backlinks from directories, bar associations, legal publications, or press mentions, the site lacks domain authority signals needed to compete with established firms. **This is a major factor in the zero rankings.**

5. **Cannibalization Risk:** The blog dashboard architecture creates a serious cannibalization risk. If every page (practice areas AND blog posts) renders as a blog listing filtered by topic, Google may see them as near-duplicate pages. The practice area pages need to clearly differentiate from blog content pages.

6. **Stale Content:** Blog posts from April 2025 (e.g., "In re Purdue Pharma LP" from April 10, 2025) and earlier may reference outdated legal developments. The Purdue Pharma case has had subsequent Supreme Court developments. **Recommendation: Audit posts older than 6 months and add update notices where law has changed.**

7. **Resources Page — Confirmed Empty.** As flagged in the instructions, `/resources` is essentially an empty page with just the blog dashboard skeleton. This is a wasted URL that could be a lead-generating asset.

8. **Security & Compliance:**
   - No privacy policy page detected
   - No cookie consent banner observed
   - ARIA labels and skip links present (basic accessibility)
   - No ADA accessibility statement
   - HTTPS implemented correctly
