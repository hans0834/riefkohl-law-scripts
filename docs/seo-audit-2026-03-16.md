## SECTION 1: CRITICAL ISSUES (Fix Immediately)

### 1.1 — JS-Dependent SEO: Googlebot Rendering Risk (CRITICAL)

**Issue:** ALL SEO fixes (H1s, meta descriptions, canonical tags, FAQ schema, hreflang) are injected via client-side JavaScript. While Google renders JS, there are known issues:
- Googlebot may not wait for `setTimeout(runFixes, 800)` or `setTimeout(fixH1, 2000)`
- Second-pass rendering adds delays to indexing
- If JS fails to load, pages have duplicate/generic H1s and Squarespace default meta descriptions

**Impact:** High — could mean Google indexes pages with wrong H1s and duplicate meta descriptions
**Recommendation:**
- Set page-level SEO titles and descriptions directly in Squarespace page settings for all core pages (this was partially done in Session 2 but needs to cover ALL pages)
- The JS overrides serve as a backup, not the primary method
- For blog posts: Squarespace blog post settings already support custom SEO title and excerpt — use these fields

### 1.5 — JS SEO Overrides Failing on Multiple Practice Area Pages (CRITICAL BUG)

**Issue:** Live testing of practice area pages reveals that the JavaScript SEO fixes are NOT reliably firing:

**Meta description override failing:** At least 3 pages (`/estate-planning`, `/business-formation`, `/government-contracts`) show the **generic sitewide meta description** instead of their configured unique ones. Googlebot may be indexing the wrong meta descriptions.

**Canonical tag bug:** Multiple practice area pages show canonical URL as `https://www.riefkohllaw.com/` (homepage!) instead of self-referencing. This tells Google these pages are duplicates of the homepage.

**H1 override unreliable on `/business-formation`:** The page may be displaying the homepage H1 ("Puerto Rico Law Firm — Trusts, Estate Planning & Business Law") instead of its configured H1.

**Duplicate H1 on `/act-60-tax-incentives`:** The blog dashboard H1 "Explore Our Legal Analysis" persists alongside the injected H1, creating two H1 tags on the page.

**Root cause:** Squarespace serves server-rendered HTML with default meta/canonical values. The JS fixes run client-side and may execute too late or not at all for crawlers.

**Impact:** CRITICAL — Google may be indexing practice area pages with wrong meta descriptions, wrong canonical URLs, and duplicate H1s
**Recommendation:**
1. Set page-level SEO titles, descriptions, and canonical URLs directly in **Squarespace Page Settings** for ALL practice area pages
2. Debug the JS timing — the `fixH1()` function may not be finding the right elements on practice area pages
3. Test each page with Google's URL Inspection tool in Search Console to see what Google actually renders
