## Email 2: The 3 Legal Blind Spots
**Send:** 3 days after Email 1
**Subject line:** The 3 legal blind spots in your Act 60 tax strategy

---

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

Your Act 60 tax strategy is probably well structured. But most decree holders have three legal blind spots that their CPA cannot fix:

**Blind Spot #1: Trust Planning**

Puerto Rico's trust law (Law 219-2012) treats a trust as an autonomous estate — a separate legal person. Mainland trusts are fiduciary relationships. This difference affects asset protection, tax treatment, and how your estate is distributed. A trust drafted for Delaware or Florida law does not automatically work under PR's civil law system.

**Blind Spot #2: Forced Heirship**

Puerto Rico reserves one-half of your estate for forced heirs — your children and surviving spouse. This is called the *legitima*. No trust, will, or beneficiary designation can override it. If your estate plan was designed for a common law state, it does not account for this rule, and your intended distributions will not be honored.

**Blind Spot #3: Decree Succession**

Your Act 60 benefits do not transfer to your heirs at death. Without specific legal structuring, your family inherits your assets but loses the tax advantages that helped build them. This is not a tax planning issue — it is an estate planning issue that requires an attorney.

Your CPA sees these problems regularly. That is why they connected you with a PR-licensed attorney who focuses on exactly these areas.

But David's estate plan was still his old Connecticut revocable trust. David's CPA referred him to a Puerto Rico attorney. Here is what the legal review revealed:

- His Connecticut trust would not protect assets from creditors under PR law the way it did on the mainland.

- His Act 60 decree benefits had no succession plan. At his death, his family would lose the tax advantages entirely.

The solution involved creating a new Puerto Rico irrevocable trust under Law 219-2012, restructuring his estate plan to comply with forced heirship while still prioritizing his wife, and implementing a decree succession strategy.

David's CPA could not have done any of this. But the CPA was the one who recognized the problem and made the referral. That is exactly how the tax-legal partnership is supposed to work.

- [ ] Trust type selected based on your goals (asset protection, estate tax reduction, or both)

**Domicile Documentation**
- [ ] PR voter registration
- [ ] PR driver's license
- [ ] PR banking relationships documented

**Decree Succession**
- [ ] Succession plan for Act 60 benefits documented
