## How to Use This Calendar

This calendar covers every major compliance deadline and recommended legal action for Act 60 decree holders in 2026. It includes obligations for both Individual Investor and Export Services decree holders.

**Color key:**
- **[TAX]** — Tax filing or payment deadline
- **[DDEC]** — Department of Economic Development and Commerce obligation
- **[LEGAL]** — Recommended legal action or review
- **[PRESENCE]** — Presence test documentation checkpoint
- **[CHARITABLE]** — Charitable donation requirement

Print this calendar. Pin it above your desk. Share it with your CPA. Never miss a deadline.

## First-Year Special: Additional Deadlines for New Decree Holders

If you received your Act 60 decree in 2025 or 2026, you have additional first-year obligations beyond the standard annual requirements.

### Within 30 Days of Decree Grant
- [ ] Review your decree terms in detail with your attorney — know every condition and deadline
- [ ] Identify your specific charitable donation amount and qualifying organizations
- [ ] Determine your employment creation timeline (if applicable to your decree)
- [ ] Begin presence test documentation from day one

### Within 6 Months of Decree Grant
- [ ] Purchase real property in Puerto Rico (if required by your decree — confirm the specific timeline in your decree terms)
- [ ] Complete domicile change documentation (driver's license, voter registration, vehicle registration, bank accounts)
- [ ] Execute a PR-compliant will and estate plan
- [ ] Form your PR business entity (if not already done)

### Within First Year of Decree Grant
- [ ] Establish an irrevocable trust under PR Law 219-2012 (recommended, even if not required)
- [ ] Complete all first-year charitable donations
- [ ] Meet all first-year employment requirements (if applicable)
- [ ] File your first DDEC annual report
- [ ] Complete your first full presence test year (183 days)
- [ ] Build and document your professional team (attorney, CPA, financial advisor)

### Common First-Year Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Waiting until year-end to track presence** — Start a daily log on January 1 (or the day your decree is granted)
2. **Assuming your mainland will works in PR** — It likely doesn't. Get a PR will immediately.
3. **Treating the property purchase deadline casually** — This is a decree condition. Missing it can trigger revocation.
4. **Making charitable donations to non-qualifying organizations** — Confirm the organization qualifies under your decree terms before donating
5. **Not documenting domicile change** — The IRS expects a paper trail. Create one from day one.

## Export Services Decree: Additional Obligations

If you hold an Export Services decree (in addition to or instead of an Individual Investor decree), add these to your calendar:

| Deadline | Obligation |
|----------|------------|
| **Ongoing** | Maintain PR-based employees as required by your decree |
| **Ongoing** | Ensure all exported services are performed from Puerto Rico |
| **Annually** | File DDEC annual report specific to Export Services compliance |
| **Annually** | Document that qualifying income was derived from services provided to clients outside PR |
| **Quarterly** | Review payroll records and employment documentation |
| **Quarterly** | Verify that business operations genuinely run from PR (office space, equipment, management decisions) |

**Key difference from Individual Investor:** Export Services compliance is operationally focused. The DDEC and IRS want to see that your business is genuinely operating in Puerto Rico — not just that you personally live here.
