# Puerto Rico Estate Planning Checklist

*Your step-by-step guide to protecting your family and assets under Puerto Rico law*

---

### How to Use This Checklist

Check off each item as you complete it. Items marked with ★ are especially critical if you relocated to Puerto Rico from the mainland. Bring this completed checklist to your estate planning consultation for a more productive meeting.

---

### Section 1: Essential Documents

- [ ] **Last will and testament** — Executed under Puerto Rico law (testamento abierto recommended)
- [ ] ★ **Review of mainland will** — If you have an existing will from another state, have it reviewed by a PR attorney for compatibility with forced heirship rules
- [ ] **Revocable trust (fideicomiso revocable)** — For probate avoidance and incapacity planning
- [ ] **Irrevocable trust (fideicomiso irrevocable)** — If estate tax planning or asset protection is needed
- [ ] **General durable power of attorney** — Designates someone to manage finances if you're incapacitated
- [ ] **Health care directive (directiva anticipada de salud)** — Documents medical treatment preferences
- [ ] **Health care proxy** — Designates someone to make medical decisions for you
- [ ] ★ **Dual-will architecture** — If you own assets in PR and mainland states, consider separate wills for each jurisdiction

### Section 2: Beneficiary Designations

- [ ] **Life insurance policies** — Verify beneficiary designations are current
- [ ] **Retirement accounts (IRA, 401k, 403b)** — Update beneficiary designations
- [ ] **Bank accounts** — Consider payable-on-death (POD) designations
- [ ] **Investment accounts** — Consider transfer-on-death (TOD) designations
- [ ] **Annuities** — Verify beneficiary designations

### Section 3: Asset Inventory

- [ ] **Real estate in Puerto Rico** — List all properties with registry information
- [ ] **Real estate outside Puerto Rico** — List mainland/international properties
- [ ] **Bank accounts** — All accounts with institutions and approximate balances
- [ ] **Investment accounts** — Brokerage accounts, stocks, bonds, mutual funds
- [ ] **Retirement accounts** — IRAs, 401ks, pensions
- [ ] **Business interests** — LLCs, corporations, partnerships
- [ ] **Life insurance** — All policies with face values
- [ ] **Vehicles** — Cars, boats, aircraft
- [ ] **Cryptocurrency** — Wallets, exchanges, access information
- [ ] **Valuable personal property** — Art, jewelry, collectibles
- [ ] **Digital assets** — Online accounts, domain names, intellectual property
- [ ] **Debts and liabilities** — Mortgages, loans, credit cards

### Section 4: Family and Beneficiary Information

- [ ] **Spouse/partner information** — Full legal name, SSN/ITIN, contact
- [ ] **Children's information** — Names, dates of birth, contact information
- [ ] ★ **Forced heirship analysis** — Understand how the legítima affects your distribution plan
- [ ] **Guardian designation for minor children** — Primary and alternate guardians named in will
- [ ] **Trustee selection** — Primary and successor trustees for any trusts
- [ ] **Executor selection** — Primary and alternate executors named in will

### Section 5: Tax Planning

- [ ] **Federal estate tax exposure** — Estimate if estate exceeds federal exemption ($15M per individual in 2026, made permanent by OBBBA)
- [ ] **Puerto Rico estate tax exposure** — Separate analysis for PR estate tax
- [ ] ★ **Act 60 / Ley 60 coordination** — If decree holder, coordinate with estate plan
- [ ] **Gift tax planning** — Consider lifetime gifting strategies
- [ ] **Charitable planning** — If applicable, explore charitable trusts or donations
- [ ] **Life insurance trust (ILIT)** — If estate tax is a concern, consider removing insurance from taxable estate

### Section 6: Special Situations

- [ ] ★ **Multi-jurisdictional assets** — Plan for assets in PR + mainland + international
- [ ] **Blended family** — Address children from prior relationships with legítima in mind
- [ ] **Special needs beneficiary** — Consider special needs trust to preserve government benefits
- [ ] **Business succession** — Plan for transfer or sale of business interests
- [ ] **Digital estate plan** — Document access to online accounts, passwords, crypto wallets

### Section 7: ★ Relocated Residents — Additional Items

- [ ] **Residency documentation** — Organize proof of PR bona fide residency (flight records, credit card statements, voter registration, driver's license)
- [ ] **Pre-move asset valuations** — Document fair market value of investments at date of PR residency establishment
- [ ] **State departure audit preparation** — If from NY, CA, CT, or other high-tax state, prepare for potential departure audit
- [ ] **Prior state estate tax analysis** — Confirm you're no longer subject to prior state's estate tax
- [ ] ★ **Mainland trust review** — Have PR attorney review any trusts created under mainland law

### Section 8: Document Organization

- [ ] **Original documents stored safely** — Fireproof safe or bank safe deposit box
- [ ] **Copies provided to attorney** — Your estate planning attorney has copies of all documents
- [ ] **Executor/trustee knows location** — Your named executor and trustee know where to find documents
- [ ] **Digital backup** — Scanned copies stored securely (encrypted cloud storage)
- [ ] **Letter of instruction** — Non-legal document with funeral wishes, account access info, and personal messages
- [ ] **Annual review scheduled** — Review estate plan annually or after major life events

---

### When to Update Your Estate Plan

Review and potentially update your plan when:
- Marriage, divorce, or separation
- Birth or adoption of a child
- Death of a beneficiary, executor, or trustee
- Significant change in asset value
- Purchase or sale of real estate
- Starting or selling a business
- Change in health status
- Move to or from Puerto Rico
- Change in tax laws (federal or PR)
- Change in Act 60 / Ley 60 decree status
